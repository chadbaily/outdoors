Why do you want to be an officer? My first year I did a few outdoors club activities, but it was last semester (my second year) that I really fell in love with the Club, via the ski crowd. The club provides such a great way for Outdoors enthusiasts to get together and learn from each other. You're never going to know everything about the Outdoors, since Mother Nature is always changing, so it's important, not to mention more fun, to learn from others. Outdoors Club at UVA provides a wonderful social network, and one that I've missed while being abroad in France this semester. In reflecting on what I miss most at UVA, and therefore what I want to put my energy into when I get back to school, Outdoors Club comes out on top. I personally want to increase my outdoors knowledge, and what better way than to be in-the-know of Outdoors at UVA! I also hope to take a Wilderness First Aid course, and I see that as one of the benefits to being an officer. I think as a girl, I can help encourage other girls to participate in the Club, as some areas are a bit male-dominated. Also, being from New York City, I wasn't exactly born in the woods (though I'm a nature girl at heart), so I'd like to encourage those who think Outdoors Club is "too intense" to at least try it out. I also hope to keep up the social aspect through pot-lucks and other social events, as I think the network is really what keeps people enthusiastic and wanting to participate. I love to do new things, so I would be up for leading hikes to places where people haven't gone before. I would also be up for managing workshops for leaders, and organizing ways to offer wilderness training courses at UVA. It seems to me like the Club has improved a lot in the past year, by encouraging people to lead, providing leader workshops, improving communication. As with all CIO's, I think it's always great to improve presence on Campus. Perhaps selling tee-shirts or even having an Outdoors Club bar night (or cook-out might be more outdoors-style) would help. I know more of the 600+ members of Outdoors Club can always get involved!
