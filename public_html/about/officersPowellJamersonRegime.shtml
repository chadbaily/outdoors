Outdoors at UVa Officers

If you have a question for the officers, please check our frequently asked questions page first. If that doesn't help, please contact us directly.

Ryan Elliott
Vice-President.
I have always loved the outdoors from early in my childhood. I was that hippy kid running around with long hair and no shoes looking at bugs, tree, stars or something. Then my years spent as a camper and staff at a summer camp in the Blue Ridge hooked me forever. I was lucky enough to grow up right at the foot of the Blue Ridge and junction of the Skyline and Parkway where I spent many days on my road bike. Recently through my time with the Outdoors Club I have become an avid backpacker, day hiker, and Teddy Graham connoisseur. I would love to add rock climbing, bouldering, and kayaking to my list of amazing stuff to do. I also need to get my lazy self back out on my mountain bike so bug me to do some Walnut Creek trips. Before all that happens though I will be setting up my slackline as often as I can escape the clutches of Clemons. There is no doubt I will be leading some sweet(at least I think so) backpacking and day hiking trips so get excited and I will see you out there.
Ryan Gilchrist
Vice-President.
I spent much of my free time growing up wandering around the woods on a mountain bike or exploring Long Island Sound in a kayak. The outdoors have always offered me everything I need, from relaxation and clearheadedness to fitness and thrillseeking. After 3 years of Outdoors at UVA giving me so much, I'm happy to be able to give something back. My club activities have primarily focused on mountain biking and snowboarding, leaving those crazy rocks to the likes of Aarash, Katrina, Billy, and Chin. It's great to be a part of this team!
Lindsay Jamerson
Co-President.
I've never been an inside girl. Growing up in the Virginia State Forest, I grew up with an appreciation of what the outdoors had to offer. It wasn't until I started going to camp, though, that I got into real outdoors sports. As a camper and, later, a camp counselor, I discovered how much I love things like rock climbing, canoeing, and hiking. Through the club, I've become a dedicated rock climber and enthusiastic (if clumsy) snowboarder. My love of the outdoors has only grown since becoming a member of Outdoors at UVA, and I'd love to help you get out there and see what beautiful Virginia has to offer!
Kelsey Kerle-O'Brien
Vice-President.
I spent the summers of my childhood camping in Alaska. It was there, among the beautiful Rocky Mountains that I gained a true love of the outdoors. In my three years at UVA so far, I have traveled to California and Ecuador to perform environmental conservation work, camp and hike. My family owns kayaks, so I enjoy paddling on the Potomac River and in the tributaries of the Occoquan at Mason Neck State Park. Although I am originally from Georgia, I am an avid skier, and I am so stoked for spring Snowshoe trips! I love to lead hikes, but I am still learning all of the trails of the Shenandoah and the surrounding area. It is my goal to attend a backpacking trip before I graduate!
Cristina Liebolt
Vice President.
Growing up in New York City, I started itching to hike to higher sights than the rocks in Central Park at a young age. I became hooked on skiing thanks to my mom's passion for the sport, and on backpacking thanks to my Maine girls' camp. I believe that inside my heart, mountains rather than skyscrapers are more my home. I have found such value in exploring the Outdoors, especially as a born and raised city girl, that I am eager to share my enthusiasm with any and everyone. Come explore the greater heights with me!
Lynn Nichols
Vice-President.
Regardless of weather, season, and location, I ache to be outside. For me, few things compare to the quiet elegance of a forest or the breathtaking views as an expansive landscape unfolds below. As a native Connecticuter, I developed a fondness for hiking, swimming, and camping at an early age and realized my passion for mountain biking midway through high school. I love the thrill of snaking between trees and racing down hillsides, not to mention the great friends I’ve made along the way. In addition to biking, I enjoy rock climbing, skiing, kayaking, stargazing, and simply adventuring wherever the trails lead. I’m so excited that the club offers so many wonderful opportunities for outdoors enthusiasts and I hope you’ll join me on a ride. Let’s hit the trails!
Becca Percy
Vice-President.
My love for the outdoors started well before my memories. Growing in the Vermont countryside, I spent far more time outdoors than watching television. I was sleeping in a tent before walking or talking, hiking before kindergarten, and backpacking in elementary school. By high school graduation I had dabbled in rock climbing. After a brief hiatus in undergrad, I have returned to all of these sports in force. Currently I am most active in rock climbing and backpacking, both attending and leading trips regularly. Other activities are berrypicking, swimming in streams, day hikes, camping, stargazing, snowshoeing, and most other outdoor activities. The mountains are one of the most relaxing and rewarding places to spend a weekend whether on rock, a mountaintop, a long hike, or a short wander. Come and relax in the woods!

I learned to fish when I was three or four, fishing led to canoeing, which led somehow to whitewater kayaking. I worked at kayaking camp (Passages) in Richmond for years, and since graduating boarding school for UVA IÕve finally had a chance to start paddling for myself. Amelia Rode
Vice-President.
Loving the outdoors is easy. I come from the northern Shenandoah Valley, living 5 miles from the Appalachian Trail. Historically, summer has been my season of outdoors romping and is when you can find me at a summer camp up in northern Michigan. Over 12 summers, I have explored many a part of Michigan, Canada and Alaska by way of backpacking, canoeing, portaging or kayaking. Now in the Outdoors Club, I have extended my adventures into all times of the year and have fallen in love with rock climbing, bouldering and the like!
Katrina Salmons
VP Emeritus, Climbing Guru.
I am compulsively, magnetically attracted to rock. I love rock and it loves me. When I was little I would beg my dad to take me bouldering, I would dream up places we'd never been. And, now thanks to our fabulous Outdoors Club I can climb any time I want. Currently I've been doing a lot of sport climbing, but hopefully soon I'll be getting into trad! When I can’t climb I might decide to camp in the woods, take a hike, clean up a highway, or just watch the stars. And lastly, my love of rock is balanced by a crazy fear of the water.
