Outdoors at UVa Officers

If you have a question for the officers, please check our frequently asked questions page first. If that doesn't help, please contact us directly.

Joe Gibson
President / Wobble Lord
As a child in the suburbs of northern Virginia, I crowned myself supreme leader of the small swath of forest behind my house. I knew every tree, root, rock, and nest in my dominion. As I grew older, I began to explore beyond my tiny kingdom to enter faraway arboreal realms on foot, mountain bike, and slackline. Here at UVA it seems the expedition never ends, be it on the Lawn or in the mountains. I'm always looking for something new to try, and new places to explore.
Helena Groves
Vice President / Ancestral Navigator
North Cackalack born and raised, I grew up blessed by the wonders of both the Outer Banks and the Blue Ridge Mountains. It was here, hunting and fishing from the earliest possible age, that I fell in love with the outdoors. Eager to explore more of my native state, I began backpacking and camping extensively throughout high school. By some random stroke of fate, I got hooked on snowsports along the way. Now I'm "on the mountain" the majority of the winter and couldn't be happier. Snowboarding gives me an excuse to be outside year round, not that you'd ever need one. Suffering from a chronic case of wanderlust, I'm constantly looking for adventure. My current excursion involves venturing in the Himalayas and the Tibetan Plateau, hopefully herding yaks and meditating on mountain precipices. Despite these ambitions to travel, Southern Appalachia will always be my home. I just can't get enough of the Bluegrass music or the BBQ. Throught OutdoorsatUVa, I look forward to gaining more experience climbing, kayaking, and mountain biking. See y'all outside!
Ben Early
Co-Chief of Fleet Management / Bongo Bodhisattva
My first road trip across the country post high school graduation really implanted my passion in the outdoors. After watching a handful of mountain biking youtubes, we strapped some makeshift bikes on the back of a car and drove out to colorado. Every summer since then, I've road tripped out west in some fashion, adventuring in everything from mountaineering to stand-up paddle boarding down glacial runoff. My goal in the Outdoors Club is to continue venturing on all sorts of whargnar expeditions from the c-ville home base. More importantly, I want to link up with as many other crazy-kewl peeps as possible in the most "fresh" OC of the land.
Kit Guncheon
Shredhorse Wrangler / Shreddy Roosevelt
Shred Shred Shred Shred. I've been shredding since day one. From the times I hit my head on the diving board to when I tried to catch a boomerang and almost blinded myself, I've always sought out thrill. Growing up, I shredded on a skateboard every day up until high school, when I started exploring the world beyond the paved paradise of Mechanicsville, Va. First exploring mountains by foot, I quickly fell in love with the human experience of conquering a towering peak. There's something special about using your own body to bring yourself to such a pristine and powerful place like a mountaintop. I've found other outlets for my need to shred in the forms of mountain biking (shredhorsing), climbing (monkey thrashing), and heelying OS (original shredder). I'm always looking to get out there and push myself closer to the edge of life. It's here I want to be. Come with me, and let's shred.

Andrews Inglis
Gear Wrangler / Vaquero
Growing up in the foothills of the Blue Ridge mountains in South Carolina, I was always getting outside. Whether it was building forts in the woods behind my house with my siblings, or going on weekend backpacking trips in the very close by North Carolina mountains, I have always felt at home in Mother Nature. Outdoors Club gives me great excuses to get outside on the weekends and try new things. While bakcpacking and hiking have always been my passion, thanks to the club I'm getting into climbing and dabbling in snow sports. Basically, being outside is the best thing ever. Try it!
Hannah Kessenich
Treasurer / Buoyancy Artisan
Seven years as a camper at Camp Green Cove in the mountains of Western North Carolina inspired my passion for whitewater canoeing and kayaking. My incredible experience at camp led me to a life-changing kayaking expedition to Chile, a summer spent teaching at the US National Whitewater Center in Charlotte, and now a summer back at camp (as a counselor this time). Outdoors at UVa has allowed me to take my kayaking to a whole new level, and to share my love of the Great Outdoors. I believe that some of the world's most beautiful places can only be reached by whitewater, and I hope to help others get there!
Charles Romero
Website Tinkerer / Elemental Conjurer
I grew up in the mountains: I lived in West Virginia until middle school, when my family moved to Colorado, and I did my undergraduate at the University of Colorado. My central passions have been skiing and fly fishing. I do miss the alpine environment of Colorado, but I've found that Virginia has its own charms too, and a lot of it is near Charlottesville! Since coming to Virginia, and joining Outdoors@UVa, I've added rock climbing to my passions. Regardless of what activity I choose, any day I can get outside is a good day, and it's made even better if I can share that with others.

Chris Winstead-Derlega
Alliance Forger / Serpent Wielder
There is a minimalism to the outdoors, simply walk OUT your DOOR. Since an early age I have been RUNNING, not walking, outdoors into the ocean, the mountains, the snow, and the forest. Growing up in Norfolk, VA, I surfed, canoed, hiked, snowboarded, and biked. Since making Charlottesville home I have leapt into road/mtn biking, kayaking, climbing, and even snowshoeing. I am always up for a classic adventure, exploring new paths, new watering holes (blue is the best), new trees or new routes. The Outdoors Club at UVA gets me to run out my door more often and into more places.
Officers Emeritus
Josh Brooks
Ex-President/Lion Rider
From smallmouth fishing in tandem canoes down the calm waters of the James, to kayaking deep into class V gorges, the river is my home and my passion. Any given weekend, you will find me near the river, either playing or photographing others as they enjoy the healing powers of this country's moving waters. OutdoorsatUVa has fostered my passion for the water, and my goal as an officer is to help others find a similar outdoor passion.
Becca Percy
Life Consultant
My love for the outdoors started well before my memories. Growing in the Vermont countryside, I spent far more time outdoors than watching television. I was sleeping in a tent before walking or talking, hiking before kindergarten, and backpacking in elementary school. By high school graduation I had dabbled in rock climbing. After a brief hiatus in undergrad, I have returned to all of these sports in force. Currently I am most active in rock climbing and backpacking, both attending and leading trips regularly. Other activities are berrypicking, swimming in streams, day hikes, camping, stargazing, snowshoeing, and most other outdoor activities. The mountains are one of the most relaxing and rewarding places to spend a weekend whether on rock, a mountaintop, a long hike, or a short wander. Come and relax in the woods!
Emily Powell
Graduate Advisor / PR Coordinator / River Otter
I've loved playing outside since I was a kid, and rivers have always had a special pull for me. I learned to fish when I was three or four; fishing led to canoeing, which in turn led to whitewater kayaking. I've taught kayaking for Passages Adventure Camp and Outdoors at UVA and for the Jackson Hole Kayak School in Wyoming. Thanks to this passion for wild rivers and places, I'm back at UVA pursuing a Master's in Environmental Planning, focusing (naturally) on watershed management. I'm thrilled to be back here, and to have the opportunity to teach kayaking for the club once again!
