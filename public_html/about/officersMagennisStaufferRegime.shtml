Outdoors at UVa Officers

If you have a question for the officers, please check our frequently asked questions page first. If that doesn't help, please contact us directly.

Chris Chin
Vice President.
I started pretty late with my outdoorsy-ness. It wasn't until I joined the Boy Scouts midway through high school that I became hooked. It all started with a bit of camping, then day hikes, then backpacking for weeks. Snowboarding was my next big adventure and I always made it a point to take advantage of what little snow we got in Northern Virginia to hit up the local slopes. After I joined the club, I went on my first climbing trip and instantly fell in love with the sport. Now I spend my time divided between my studies, longboarding, snowboarding, and climbing with the occasional camping or hiking trip. I hope to get a chance to get to know everyone on our trips. Remember, Thomas Jefferson wanted you to do it outdoors!
Billy Kohlbrenner
Vice-President.
Growing up in Northern Virginia is not necessarily a recipe for really falling in love with the backcountry and all it has to offer, but somehow for me it worked out just fine. I started young, camping with the Boy Scouts, backpacking with my father and brother, and by age 12 I started climbing. By 15, I loved the outdoors so much that I never spent a single summer of high school at home. I would be backpacking for months or working as a climbing instructor and caving director for a high adventure summer program. During high school though, I really didn't have time during the school year to do all of the climbing and hiking I loved so much. I suddenly had a lot more time on my hands when I came to UVA, and the club was an open door for me. I love climbing and I really believe that you can see all the world has to offer while hanging from a rope.
Meghan Magennis
Co-President.
There's no place a car will take you that looks like the top of a mountain or the middle of a river! I love being in the woods and fresh air; hiking and backpacking are my favorite activities. In addition to exploring local trails in Shenandoah National Park, I've been to the White Mountains, Joshua Tree, and the Grand Canyon with the club. In addition to land activities, I hope to add paddling to my list this year. This club is a wonderful resource for learning and sharing great skills so don't hesitate to sign up for a trip! It's also easy to lead a trip, so please email me with any questions!

Katrina Salmons
VP Emeritus, Climbing Guru.
I am compulsively, magnetically attracted to rock. I love rock and it loves me. When I was little I would beg my dad to take me bouldering, I would dream up places we'd never been. And, now thanks to our fabulous Outdoors Club I can climb any time I want. Currently I've been doing a lot of sport climbing, but hopefully soon I'll be getting into trad! When I can’t climb I might decide to camp in the woods, take a hike, clean up a highway, or just watch the stars. And lastly, my love of rock is balanced by a crazy fear of the water.
Aarash Sofla
President Emeritus, Head Climbing Instructor.
For 10 years before coming to UVa I was barely seen in towns on weekends. Moving around hot deserts to crevassed glaciers, boulders to big walls, seaside to high altitude. My favorites are alpine climbing, high altitude mountaineering, aid climbing and recently skydiving. In 1999 I was certified as a climbing trainer and mountain guide. Here in the club I serve as a climbing instructor-advisor. We think to have the best climbing team of Virginia here at UVa. Let's do it.
Eric Stauffer
Co-President.
My passion for the outdoors began at a young age, when I started hiking with my dad. I later attended an outdoor adventure camp for a few weeks every summer during my early teenage years. It was there, in the mountains of West Virginia, where I first tried backpacking, rafting, climbing, caving, mountain biking, and kayaking. I was hooked and haven't looked back since. Lately, climbing has become my favorite outdoor pursuit. I also really enjoy hiking, backpacking, mountain biking, scuba diving, or simply relaxing outside. Feel free to email me about leading any trips, either in the local area or beyond!
Brian Tai
Vice-President.
I am living proof that you can be a hardcore engineer and still get outside. I rotate my time between being a code ninja, a yeti on the mountain, and a guy lost in the woods pretty regularly. I always say I'll try anything twice, and am a fan of others who share that belief. I may not know the absolute best route to the next geo-cache but I'm pretty sure I'll have the most fun getting there.
Lila Warren
Vice-President.
I have been an outdoor enthusiast since birth! I grew up in the countryside of Virginia, canoeing, fishing, and hiking with my family; I also started riding horses and snowboarding at an early age. In high school I went on an Outward Bound course in North Carolina, and also learned the basics of snowshoeing and glacier mountaineering on a trip to the Adirondacks of New York. My favorite outdoor activities are backpacking, snowboarding, horseback riding, and, most recently, flyfishing. Although I'm a country girl at heart, I also love the beach. Let me know if you have any questions or new ideas for trips - and, if you're ever up for an off-the-beaten-path adventure, I'm game. I have been in love with the outdoors from square one. Happy Trails!
Bob Weaver
Vice-President.
I became an outdoorsman at the age of six months on my first camping trip. Ever since I have loved every type of outdoors activity I have tried. When I was eight, I discovered that skiing is the greatest activity of all time. During high school I found mountain biking to be a close second. I also love backpacking, hiking, climbing, rafting, camping, kayaking, trail running, slacklining, stargazing, mountaineering, snowshoeing, and basically everything else – especially if it’s cold out and the world is pure and white. Life is just too short to be spent in libraries – the best learning happens around a campfire with good friends or people you just met. I hope to introduce you to some of my passions and to learn some new ones from you.
