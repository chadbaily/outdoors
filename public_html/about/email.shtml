Outdoors at UVa Email List Policy

The main purpose of the outdoors-club list is for outdoor and club-related email. If the email is in keeping with our mission statement, and you feel that it is something that 500 or more other members would benefit from reading, then go ahead, send the email, and help build our outdoor community. Please ask the officers if you are in doubt. We are not super-strict, but we do want to keep our club focused on the things mentioned in our mission statement. Here are some rough guidelines:

Appropriate emails include:

  • Anything involving the outdoors
  • Notices about outdoorsy presentations
  • Information about Skyline Drive, the Blue Ridge Parkway, conditions of local rivers and hikes, etc.
  • Non club related trips (i.e. anyone want to join me on a hike to...)
  • Looking for climbing partners, running partners, someone to go on a spur-of-the-moment hike or other activity

This list is not exhaustive. All of these items build our community and are related to our mission.

Inappropriate emails are those which are not outdoorsy and not related to our mission, such as:

  • Looking for a ride home
  • Advertising anything for sale (use the classified ads section of the website to sell your outdoorsy gear)
  • Advertising other groups or organizations (please contact the officers if you have questions)
  • Commentary on outside issues such as politics or world events
  • Elections or endorsements
  • Announcements of non-outdoorsy social activities or sports teams (parties, looking for people to join an intramural team, etc.)
  • Porn, jokes, chain letters

Email Writing Guidelines

Please keep your email short and to the point. If you need to make it long, it's probably better to just mention the main point of the email in one or two sentences, and then give your name and contact information so people can get in touch with you directly to find out more. Please follow these guidelines:

  • Keep it short. Remember that you are emailing 500+ people.
  • DO NOT SHOUT. EVER. THIS IS SHOUTING.
  • Don't use lots of exclamation points, either!!!
  • Create a relevant, short subject.

Here is a sample email:

Subject:Climb at Rocky Top Thursday

I'm going climbing at Rocky Top Thursday at 5:00 PM. Contact Morgan Jones at , or 123-4567, if you want to ride along.

If you're not sure about an email you want to send, please ask the officers.
