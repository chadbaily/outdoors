Outdoors at UVa Officers

If you have a question for the officers, please check our frequently asked questions page first. If that doesn't help, please contact us directly.

Chris Chin
Vice President.
I started pretty late with my outdoorsy-ness. It wasn't until I joined the Boy Scouts midway through high school that I became hooked. It all started with a bit of camping, then day hikes, then backpacking for weeks. Snowboarding was my next big adventure and I always made it a point to take advantage of what little snow we got in Northern Virginia to hit up the local slopes. After I joined the club, I went on my first climbing trip and instantly fell in love with the sport. Now I spend my time divided between my studies, longboarding, snowboarding, and climbing with the occasional camping or hiking trip. I hope to get a chance to get to know everyone on our trips. Remember, Thomas Jefferson wanted you to do it outdoors!
Ryan Gilchrist

I spent much of my free time growing up wandering around the woods on a mountain bike or exploring Long Island Sound in a kayak. The outdoors have always offered me everything I need, from relaxation and clearheadedness to fitness and thrillseeking. After 3 years of Outdoors at UVA giving me so much, I'm happy to be able to give something back. My club activities have primarily focused on mountain biking and snowboarding, leaving those crazy rocks to the likes of Aarash, Katrina, Billy, and Chin. It's great to be a part of this team!

My first experience with outdoorsyness was with Camp Piamingo when I was in elementary school. Let's just say I rocked the ropes course. Even the big fat seals are pretty in their own way. I've discovered I really love mountain biking, and I've always been a reasonably good skier. I'm just now immersing myself in all of the great outdoors activities around UVa and I hope you'll join me!

Becca Percy
Vice-President.
My love for the outdoors started well before my memories. Growing in the Vermont countryside, I spent far more time outdoors than watching television. I was sleeping in a tent before walking or talking, hiking before kindergarten, and backpacking in elementary school. By high school graduation I had dabbled in rock climbing. After a brief hiatus in undergrad, I have returned to all of these sports in force. Currently I am most active in rock climbing and backpacking, both attending and leading trips regularly. Other activities are berrypicking, swimming in streams, day hikes, camping, stargazing, snowshoeing, and most other outdoor activities. The mountains are one of the most relaxing and rewarding places to spend a weekend whether on rock, a mountaintop, a long hike, or a short wander. Come and relax in the woods!

Katrina Salmons
VP Emeritus, Climbing Guru.
I am compulsively, magnetically attracted to rock. I love rock and it loves me. When I was little I would beg my dad to take me bouldering, I would dream up places we'd never been. And, now thanks to our fabulous Outdoors Club I can climb any time I want. Currently I've been doing a lot of sport climbing, but hopefully soon I'll be getting into trad! When I can’t climb I might decide to camp in the woods, take a hike, clean up a highway, or just watch the stars. And lastly, my love of rock is balanced by a crazy fear of the water.

Lila Warren
Co-President.
I have been an outdoor enthusiast since birth! I grew up in the countryside of Virginia, canoeing, fishing, and hiking with my family; I also started riding horses and snowboarding at an early age. In high school I went on an Outward Bound course in North Carolina, and also learned the basics of snowshoeing and mountaineering on a trip to the Adirondacks. My favorite outdoor activities are backpacking, snowboarding, trail-running, horseback riding, and, most recently, flyfishing. Although I'm a country girl at heart, I also love the beach. Let me know if you have any questions or new ideas for trips - and, if you're ever up for an off-the-beaten-path adventure, I'm game. I have been in love with the outdoors from square one - join me there. Happy Trails!
Bob Weaver
Co-President.
I became an outdoorsman at the age of six months on my first camping trip. Ever since I have loved every type of outdoors activity I have tried. When I was eight, I discovered that skiing is the greatest activity of all time. During high school I found mountain biking to be a close second. I also love backpacking, hiking, climbing, rafting, camping, kayaking, trail running, slacklining, stargazing, mountaineering, snowshoeing, and basically everything else  especially if its cold out and the world is pure and white. Life is just too short to be spent in libraries  the best learning happens around a campfire with good friends or people you just met. I hope to introduce you to some of my passions and to learn some new ones from you.
